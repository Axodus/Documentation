# AXODUS DAO FEDERATION MODEL

Version: 0.1 Draft  
Status: Architecture Document

---

## 1. Overview

Axodus operates as a federation of autonomous DAOs connected through a shared constitutional, economic and governance framework.

Each DAO operates independently while remaining aligned with the Axodus Constitution and Root Governance.

---

## 2. Federation Structure

```txt
Axodus Root DAO
    ↓
Partner DAOs
    ↓
Members / Clients / Agents / Operators
```

---

## 3. Root DAO

The Root DAO is responsible for:

- constitutional governance;
- ecosystem economic coordination;
- product access rules;
- treasury standards;
- security standards;
- ecosystem expansion;
- governance registry;
- partner DAO validation.

---

## 4. Partner DAO Structure

Each DAO should contain:

- identity;
- governance configuration;
- treasury;
- member/client registry;
- product permissions;
- agents;
- investment strategies;
- reporting layer;
- constitutional standing.

---

## 5. DAO Creation Flow

1. Create DAO
2. Configure governance
3. Accept Axodus Constitution
4. Initialize treasury
5. Register DAO in Axodus federation
6. Assign product permissions
7. Activate operational dashboard
8. Begin proposals and execution

---

## 6. DAO Types

### Investment DAO
Focused on treasury growth and asset allocation.

### Service DAO
Focused on services, client operations and commercial activity.

### Trading DAO
Focused on trading strategies and capital management.

### Media / BBA DAO
Focused on marketing, communication and business growth.

### Mining DAO
Focused on hashrate acquisition and mining operations.

### Compute DAO
Focused on GPU providers, inference infrastructure and compute leasing.

### Academy DAO
Focused on education, certification, curation and Proof of Knowledge.

---

## 7. Product Access Model

All capital-sensitive products should be accessed through DAO governance.

Access flow:

```txt
DAO Treasury
    ↓
Proposal
    ↓
Voting
    ↓
Execution
    ↓
Product Contract / Backend Adapter
    ↓
Receipt / Report
```

---

## 8. Federation Standing

Each DAO should have a federation status:

- `pending`
- `active`
- `restricted`
- `under-review`
- `suspended`
- `sanctioned`

Each DAO should also have a constitutional standing:

- `compliant`
- `warning`
- `critical`
- `constitutional-breach`

---

## 9. Revenue Model

Revenue may originate from:

- commissions;
- performance fees;
- marketplace activity;
- product fees;
- staking rewards;
- trading operations;
- mining yield;
- compute leasing;
- agent subscriptions;
- DAO onboarding;
- BBA services.

---

## 10. Reward Distribution

Each DAO may define:

- member participation rules;
- share distribution;
- fee allocation;
- treasury reserve;
- reinvestment rules;
- operator compensation.

These rules must remain compatible with the Constitution.

---

## 11. Agent Integration

DAOs may operate autonomous agents for:

- trading;
- governance analysis;
- treasury management;
- infrastructure operations;
- marketing;
- client support;
- risk review.

---

## 12. Scalability Model

Axodus scales horizontally by enabling new DAOs to join the federation and operate products under shared standards.

---

## 13. Long-Term Objective

To establish a decentralized network of autonomous economic organizations operating under shared constitutional governance.
