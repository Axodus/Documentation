## Purpose

This folder contains the deep semantic knowledge base for the Axodus Marketplace nucleus.

These files are not public-facing documentation pages. They are agent-oriented knowledge packs designed to help coding agents, documentation agents, ACS agents, Business operators, Academy operators, BBA reviewers, governance reviewers, treasury reviewers, product planners, security reviewers, and future MCP systems generate accurate and consistent public documentation and implementation plans.

Axodus Marketplace is the commercial exchange layer of the Axodus ecosystem.

It supports the discovery, listing, purchase, settlement, delivery, review, and governance-aware commercialization of products, services, courses, templates, digital assets, integrations, DAO services, BBA services, Academy content, and future ecosystem-native offerings.

## Marketplace Thesis

Axodus Marketplace is not a speculative token shop.

Axodus Marketplace is a trust-oriented, governance-aware, token-integrated, service-aware, and education-connected commercial layer.

It exists to help users, tutors, creators, DAOs, businesses, service providers, and Axodus product nuclei commercialize useful offerings while preserving:

- listing quality;
- seller verification;
- buyer clarity;
- transparent fees;
- settlement accountability;
- dispute handling;
- token utility;
- locked reward rules;
- treasury reporting;
- governance control for sensitive categories;
- responsible product claims;
- integration with Academy, Business, BBA, DEX, DeFi, Tokenomics, Treasury, and ACS.

## Strategic Role in Axodus

Marketplace is one of the main bridges between product utility and real economic activity.

It can support:

- Academy course commerce;
- tutor and creator offerings;
- Business service packages;
- BBA agency services;
- DAO service requests;
- templates and digital assets;
- Web3 product services;
- integrations and MCP services;
- access to product subscriptions;
- internal ecosystem purchases;
- `$Neurons` utility;
- locked reward internal spending if policy and contracts allow;
- DEX-assisted payment conversion if implemented;
- treasury revenue and fee routing.

Marketplace must be documented carefully because commerce, token payments, locked rewards, refunds, seller settlement, quality claims, and service delivery create financial, operational, and trust risks.

## Non-Negotiable Marketplace Invariants

- Public Marketplace documentation must be written in English.
- Marketplace must not promise guaranteed seller revenue, guaranteed buyer outcomes, guaranteed ROI, guaranteed course value, guaranteed trading profit, or guaranteed token appreciation.
- Listings must not make unsupported claims.
- Paid courses, services, subscriptions, rewards, and locked credits must have clear rules.
- Locked rewards from free Academy paths must not be described as transferable or withdrawable unless contracts and policy explicitly allow it.
- Locked rewards can only be described as Marketplace-spendable if policy, contracts, treasury, seller settlement, and compliance model support it.
- Seller/tutor/provider status must be clear: unverified, verified, approved, suspended, deprecated, or official.
- Payment, fee, refund, delivery, and settlement rules must be transparent.
- DEX-assisted checkout must not be claimed unless implemented and reviewed.
- Treasury must report marketplace fees, seller settlement, refunds, credits, liabilities, and revenue.
- Governance must review sensitive listing categories, fee policy, token utility, locked reward usage, dispute policy, and seller standards.
- BBA must review public listing and campaign claims when sensitive.
- ACS supports analysis, review, and reporting but does not approve listings, settle disputes, move funds, or override governance alone.
