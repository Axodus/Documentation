## Purpose

This folder contains the deep semantic knowledge base for the Axodus Mining nucleus.

These files are not public-facing documentation pages. They are agent-oriented knowledge packs designed to help coding agents, documentation agents, ACS agents, infrastructure operators, governance reviewers, treasury reviewers, security reviewers, compliance reviewers, product planners, and future MCP systems generate accurate and consistent public documentation and implementation plans.

Axodus Mining is the ecosystem nucleus responsible for mining-related infrastructure, mining-product design, reward accounting, operational monitoring, treasury alignment, user education, and responsible public documentation.

Because "Mining" can mean different things in Web3, this knowledge pack defines Axodus Mining broadly and carefully. It may include:

- Proof-of-Work mining operations;
- hashrate allocation and pool participation;
- mining infrastructure management;
- mining reward accounting;
- validator or node operations where the network model is not Proof-of-Work;
- user-facing mining access products;
- mining subscriptions or plans if approved;
- marketplace mining services or templates;
- treasury mining exposure;
- energy, hardware, uptime, and operational risk;
- reporting and accountability for mined rewards, costs, and payouts.

## Mining Thesis

Axodus Mining is not a guaranteed-income product.

Axodus Mining is an infrastructure and product nucleus designed to manage mining or validation exposure responsibly, transparently, and with clear risk controls.

Mining can generate rewards, but rewards are variable and depend on factors such as:

- network difficulty;
- asset price;
- energy cost;
- hardware performance;
- pool performance;
- uptime;
- maintenance;
- block rewards and fees;
- validator performance where applicable;
- slashing or penalty rules where applicable;
- market liquidity;
- tax, regulatory, and jurisdictional constraints.

Therefore, Mining documentation must never promise guaranteed profit, guaranteed daily returns, guaranteed ROI, passive income, fixed yield, or risk-free participation.

## Strategic Role in Axodus

Mining can serve several ecosystem roles:

- provide a product entry point for users who want simple exposure to mining-like infrastructure;
- create educational onboarding through Academy;
- support Treasury through infrastructure-backed revenue if approved;
- create Marketplace listings for mining-related services, equipment coordination, dashboards, templates, or subscriptions if approved;
- provide Business service packages for mining infrastructure planning or monitoring;
- provide BBA campaign opportunities under strict claim review;
- connect to Trading for hedging, asset management, and revenue conversion;
- connect to Tokenomics if `$Neurons` is used for access, rewards, discounts, governance, or payments;
- connect to Governance for policy, risk limits, product activation, treasury allocation, and parameter updates;
- connect to ACS for monitoring, risk summaries, reports, and claim review.

## Non-Negotiable Mining Invariants

- Public Mining documentation must be written in English.
- Mining documentation must never promise guaranteed profit, guaranteed ROI, guaranteed payout, guaranteed APY, guaranteed daily income, or risk-free participation.
- Mining rewards must be described as variable and dependent on network, operational, market, hardware, pool, validator, energy, and regulatory factors.
- Mining products must clearly separate simulation, estimate, testnet, pilot, and active status.
- Profitability calculators must be labeled as estimates, not guarantees.
- User-facing mining products require clear disclosures, eligibility rules, payout rules, fees, withdrawal rules, and risk notes.
- Treasury mining exposure requires treasury policy, governance review, risk limits, and reporting.
- Hardware, hosting, pool, validator, or custody relationships must be explicit and not overclaimed.
- Energy, sustainability, uptime, and maintenance assumptions must be documented where relevant.
- Node or validator operations must disclose slashing, downtime, delegation, custody, and network rule risks where applicable.
- Marketplace mining services must not overpromise results.
- BBA must review public marketing claims.
- ACS supports analysis and monitoring but does not approve mining products, payouts, treasury allocations, or public claims alone.
