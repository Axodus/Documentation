## 1. Tokenomics Identity

tokenomics_role:
  primary:
    - economic_design_layer
    - token_utility_policy_layer
    - reward_policy_layer
    - access_and_participation_model
    - treasury_alignment_layer
    - governance_controlled_incentive_system
    - risk_and_sustainability_layer

tokenomics_is_not:
  - investment_promise
  - guaranteed_yield_system
  - passive_income_scheme
  - price_appreciation_claim
  - unrestricted_reward_minting_machine
  - marketing_hype_layer
  - substitute_for_governance
  - substitute_for_contract_validation
  - legal_or_regulatory_certification

tokenomics_thesis:
  - Axodus Tokenomics defines how `$Neurons` and related incentive mechanisms support the ecosystem.
  - The token should be documented through utility, rewards, access, governance participation, Marketplace usage, Academy progression, and ecosystem coordination.
  - Tokenomics must be sustainable, transparent, governance-aware, contract-validated, and risk-aware.
  - Every reward or utility claim must map to policy, implementation, and accountability.
  - Tokenomics must avoid speculative or investment language.

## 10. Public Documentation Guidance

When generating public Tokenomics docs:

- Use `$Neurons` consistently.
- Explain utility before rewards.
- Explain rewards as policy-governed and contract-dependent.
- Clearly separate active, planned, experimental, and draft behavior.
- Include risk language on every reward, fee, governance, treasury, and marketplace page.
- Avoid price, profit, APY, yield, or income promises.
- Link Tokenomics docs to Academy, Marketplace, Governance, Treasury, ACS, Business, and Accountability.
