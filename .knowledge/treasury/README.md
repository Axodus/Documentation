## Purpose

This folder contains the deep semantic knowledge base for the Treasury nucleus of Axodus.

These files are not public-facing documentation pages. They are agent-oriented knowledge packs designed to help coding agents, ACS agents, Trinity, governance reviewers, risk reviewers, treasury operators, documentation agents, and future MCP systems generate accurate and consistent public documentation and implementation plans.

The Treasury nucleus defines the financial sustainability, capital allocation, reserve policy, risk management, revenue routing, strategy oversight, reporting, and accountability layer of Axodus.

## Treasury Thesis

Axodus Treasury is the financial responsibility layer of the ecosystem.

Treasury is not a speculative fund, not a profit promise, and not a hidden wallet.

Treasury exists to preserve sustainability, operational continuity, risk discipline, transparent reporting, governance-aligned capital allocation, and accountable financial decision-making.

Treasury connects:

- Governance;
- Trading;
- Tokenomics;
- Business revenue;
- Marketplace fees;
- Academy reward budgets;
- DaaS / DeFi strategy policy;
- liquidity and reserve management;
- operating expenses;
- risk controls;
- security and custody;
- reporting and accountability.

## Core Treasury Responsibilities

Treasury must help answer:

- Where does ecosystem revenue go?
- Which assets are held?
- Which reserves are required?
- Which strategies are allowed?
- How much exposure is acceptable?
- Who can approve treasury actions?
- Which actions require governance?
- How are losses, gains, fees, rewards, and liabilities reported?
- How are treasury risks controlled?
- How are tokenomics decisions connected to financial sustainability?

## Non-Negotiable Treasury Invariants

- Treasury documentation must never promise profit, fixed returns, guaranteed APY, or risk-free yield.
- Treasury must be governance-aligned.
- Treasury must be transparent enough for accountability while protecting sensitive operational security.
- Treasury capital allocation must have policy, authority, limits, records, and reporting.
- Trading-related treasury activity requires risk limits and reporting.
- Tokenomics-related treasury flows require reportability.
- Marketplace credits, locked rewards, fees, and reward budgets may create treasury implications and must be reviewed.
- Custody, keys, wallets, multisigs, and permissions require security controls.
- Material treasury actions require governance visibility, review, or approval.
- ACS/Trinity supports analysis and reporting but does not approve or execute treasury actions alone.
- Public Treasury documentation must be written in English.
