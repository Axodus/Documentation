[AXODUS_PHILOSOPHY]

philosophical_position:
  core_statement:
    - Axodus exists to build DeFi infrastructure that is transparent, sustainable, modular, and accountable
    - Axodus rejects opaque financial promises and speculative-only narratives
    - Axodus treats governance, education, and risk management as foundational infrastructure rather than secondary features

why_axodus_exists:
  problems:
    - DeFi often lacks clear accountability
    - users are exposed to tools without sufficient education
    - financial products are frequently marketed with unrealistic yield expectations
    - DAO governance can become symbolic instead of operational
    - treasury strategies are often opaque
    - AI automation can become unsafe without governance constraints
    - ecosystem products can fragment without a shared architecture
  response:
    - create a governance-centered ecosystem
    - connect products through constitutional principles
    - educate users before advanced financial exposure
    - document decisions and financial activity
    - use agents as support systems, not unchecked authorities
    - create modular nuclei with clear roles

core_values:
  transparency:
    meaning:
      - users, contributors, and governance participants should understand how systems operate
      - financial flows should be explainable
      - governance records should be traceable
  sustainability:
    meaning:
      - long-term ecosystem health is prioritized over short-term hype
      - treasury and product strategies must consider risk and continuity
  accountability:
    meaning:
      - decisions should produce records
      - treasury actions should be reportable
      - product releases should be documented
  decentralization:
    meaning:
      - governance should enable participation
      - DAO structures should reduce unchecked centralized control where possible
      - decentralization must be practical and auditable, not only rhetorical
  education:
    meaning:
      - users should understand tools before using them
      - Academy is part of risk mitigation
      - Proof of Knowledge can support responsible access
  modularity:
    meaning:
      - each nucleus has clear responsibility
      - products can evolve independently while remaining aligned
  risk_awareness:
    meaning:
      - risk must be explicit
      - uncertainty must be documented
      - no financial tool is risk-free
  legitimacy:
    meaning:
      - important ecosystem decisions require appropriate governance, review, or accountability
  auditability:
    meaning:
      - records, contracts, proposals, reports, and releases should be inspectable when possible

communication_style:
  preferred:
    - clear
    - direct
    - institutional
    - educational
    - realistic
    - technically grounded
    - risk-aware
  avoid:
    - hype
    - vague promises
    - guaranteed returns
    - exaggerated decentralization claims
    - unsupported partnership claims
    - unsupported audit claims
    - unclear token speculation

economic_philosophy:
  principles:
    - token utility should come from ecosystem participation
    - rewards should be aligned with productive behavior
    - treasury strategies must be risk-managed
    - trading products must not promise profit
    - educational incentives should support onboarding and quality participation
    - marketplace utility should support real consumption
  forbidden_claims:
    - guaranteed APY
    - guaranteed profit
    - risk-free yield
    - passive income certainty
    - guaranteed token appreciation
    - guaranteed treasury performance

governance_philosophy:
  principles:
    - governance is coordination infrastructure
    - governance must connect decisions to execution
    - governance should not be purely symbolic
    - governance must respect constitutional constraints
    - local DAOs may have autonomy but not unlimited authority
    - federation requires alignment and traceability
  risks:
    - capture
    - low participation
    - opaque execution
    - plugin misuse
    - treasury abuse
    - fake decentralization
  mitigations:
    - layered governance
    - public records
    - constitutional guardrails
    - risk review
    - accountability reports
    - ACS-assisted analysis with human and governance validation

agent_philosophy:
  principles:
    - agents are operational support systems
    - agents can analyze, classify, summarize, challenge, monitor, and suggest
    - agents must not become unbounded financial or governance authorities
    - critical decisions require human, technical, or governance review
  agent_roles:
    morpheus:
      essence:
        - strategy
        - narrative coherence
        - governance alignment
    trinity:
      essence:
        - trading intelligence
        - operations
        - execution analysis
        - risk monitoring
    agent_smith:
      essence:
        - adversarial review
        - stress testing
        - friction
        - abuse resistance

documentation_philosophy:
  principles:
    - documentation is infrastructure
    - documentation must evolve with the project
    - old documentation must not remain canonical if the project changed
    - every major concept should have purpose, scope, current status, risks, and future work
    - knowledge packs support agents
    - public docs support humans
    - planning documents support internal evolution

critical_invariants:
  - clarity_over_hype
  - accountability_over_opacity
  - governance_over_uncontrolled_execution
  - education_over_blind_access
  - sustainability_over_unsustainable_yield
  - utility_over_speculation
  - review_over_unchecked_automation
  - modularity_over_monolithic_design
