[AXODUS_CONSTITUTIONAL_MODEL]

constitutional_definition:
  summary:
    - the Axodus Constitution is the shared rule and value framework that binds the ecosystem
    - it defines principles, constraints, legitimacy requirements, and governance expectations
    - it protects Axodus from fragmented, opaque, unsafe, or misaligned growth
  current_status:
    - conceptually defined
    - should be documented as draft unless final governance ratification exists
    - must not be presented as legally finalized unless confirmed

constitutional_purpose:
  - define ecosystem values
  - establish governance constraints
  - guide DAO federation
  - regulate product access
  - protect treasury accountability
  - prevent unsafe or misleading financial activity
  - ensure transparency and traceability
  - align local autonomy with ecosystem-wide responsibility

constitutional_scope:
  applies_to:
    - Executive DAO
    - Boardroom Council
    - Community DAO
    - federated DAOs
    - local DAOs
    - product nuclei
    - treasury operations
    - business service requests
    - ACS-supported workflows
    - tokenomics and reward systems
    - accountability requirements
  does_not_replace:
    - legal compliance
    - security audits
    - smart contract verification
    - human accountability
    - jurisdiction-specific requirements
    - risk management policies

constitutional_principles:
  transparency:
    - decisions should be explainable
    - records should be accessible where appropriate
    - treasury and governance activity should be traceable
  sustainability:
    - ecosystem growth must avoid unsustainable promises
    - yield and reward models must respect risk
  governance_legitimacy:
    - significant decisions require proper governance routing
    - execution should follow documented approval paths
  local_autonomy_with_constraints:
    - local DAOs can define local workflows
    - local DAOs can request custom plugins
    - local DAOs cannot violate ecosystem guardrails
  accountability:
    - financial, governance, and product activities should produce reports or records
  risk_management:
    - financial exposure must be reviewed
    - trading and treasury strategies must include risk controls
  education:
    - user onboarding and knowledge should be treated as part of responsible access
  agentic_limits:
    - AI agents may assist but not become final unchecked authorities

constitutional_governance_layers:
  executive_dao:
    constitutional_role:
      - strategic coordination
      - roadmap alignment
      - executive execution layer
      - technical prioritization
      - internal ecosystem stewardship
  boardroom_council:
    constitutional_role:
      - supervisory validation
      - risk and compliance review
      - treasury oversight
      - constitutional interpretation support
      - escalation layer
  community_dao:
    constitutional_role:
      - public legitimacy
      - open participation
      - proposal signaling
      - community voting
      - ecosystem accountability pressure

federation_model:
  definition:
    - a network of DAOs aligned under shared constitutional principles
    - local DAOs may operate independently in local matters
    - access to official Axodus infrastructure depends on alignment
  hub:
    - canonical Axodus governance
    - constitutional authority
    - federation registry
    - ecosystem-wide guardrails
  spokes:
    - local DAOs
    - product DAOs
    - partner DAOs
    - community-specific DAOs
    - sub-DAOs
  requirements:
    - constitutional alignment
    - traceability
    - governance visibility
    - compliance with product access rules
    - risk and treasury constraints when applicable

constitutional_guardrails:
  financial:
    - no guaranteed profit claims
    - no risk-free yield claims
    - treasury actions require governance or authorized policy
    - high-risk products require disclosure
  governance:
    - proposals must be traceable
    - execution must be recorded
    - local plugins must not bypass constitutional constraints
  technical:
    - security-sensitive deployments require review
    - smart contracts require audit or clear unaudited status
    - API and wallet security must be documented
  tokenomics:
    - token utility must not be misrepresented as guaranteed investment return
    - reward flows must reflect contract or policy status
    - locked rewards must be described accurately
  agentic:
    - AI agents cannot unilaterally approve treasury execution
    - AI agents cannot override governance
    - AI outputs must be reviewable

product_access_logic:
  principle:
    - official Axodus product access should depend on governance-defined eligibility, constitutional alignment, and operational requirements
  examples:
    - a DAO may access a governance plugin only after review
    - a sub-DAO may request a custom workflow through Business
    - Academy reward rules may require governance validation
    - Trading strategy access may depend on token holdings, subscription, milestones, or governance-defined rules
  caution:
    - do not document any access rule as final unless implemented and approved

proposal_alignment:
  every_relevant_proposal_should_check:
    - constitutional alignment
    - governance authority
    - treasury impact
    - risk classification
    - technical feasibility
    - accountability requirements
    - affected nuclei
    - implementation status
    - required approvals

constitutional_anti_patterns:
  - local_DAO_bypasses_core_guardrails
  - treasury_execution_without_traceability
  - governance_vote_without_execution_record
  - product_launch_without_risk_context
  - ACS_agent_presented_as_final_authority
  - token_reward_presented_as_guaranteed_financial_return
  - undocumented_change_request_affecting_scope_or_cost
  - fake_decentralization_without_real_accountability

documentation_rules:
  - describe the Constitution as a governance framework
  - mark status as Draft unless ratified
  - distinguish constitutional principles from legal claims
  - avoid claiming legal enforceability unless confirmed
  - link constitutional model to governance, business, treasury, ACS, and accountability
