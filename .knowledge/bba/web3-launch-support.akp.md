## 1. Launch Support Thesis

Web3 launches are high-risk communication moments.

A launch can affect user expectations, governance legitimacy, token perception, security risk, product adoption, and community trust.

BBA launch support must ensure the launch is clear, claim-reviewed, risk-aware, and aligned with product readiness.

## 3. Launch Readiness Checklist

readiness_checklist:
  - product_status_confirmed
  - target_audience_defined
  - launch_objective_defined
  - key_messages_defined
  - public_claims_reviewed
  - risk_disclosures_prepared
  - docs_or_guides_available
  - support_or_FAQ_ready
  - governance_approval_if_required
  - security_or_audit_claims_verified
  - tokenomics_claims_verified
  - trading_claims_reviewed_if_relevant
  - metrics_defined
  - incident_or_pause_plan_defined_if_relevant

## 5. Launch Risk Controls

risk_controls:
  - avoid_unverified_claims
  - mark_beta_or_experimental_status
  - include_risk_disclosures
  - avoid_token_price_language
  - avoid_trading_profit_promises
  - avoid_security_overclaims
  - provide_clear_next_steps
  - ensure_support_path_exists
  - monitor_feedback_after_launch

## 6. Public Documentation Guidance

The public Web3 Launch Support page should:

- explain launch types;
- define readiness checklist;
- list launch assets;
- explain risk controls;
- link to Claim Review, Campaign Lifecycle, Growth, Governance, and Reporting.
