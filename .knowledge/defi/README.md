## Purpose

This folder contains the deep semantic knowledge base for the Axodus DeFi nucleus.

These files are not public-facing documentation pages. They are agent-oriented knowledge packs designed to help coding agents, documentation agents, ACS agents, governance reviewers, treasury reviewers, risk reviewers, smart contract reviewers, and future MCP systems generate accurate and consistent public documentation and implementation plans.

The DeFi nucleus defines the decentralized finance product and protocol layer of Axodus.

It includes the conceptual, strategic, operational, governance, risk, treasury, tokenomics, and security framework for:

- DeFi as a Service;
- ETF DaaS;
- staking and yield models;
- vault-like structures;
- liquidity strategies;
- DeFi integrations;
- protocol risk;
- treasury strategy exposure;
- tokenomics utility and reward flows;
- governance-controlled parameters;
- risk reporting and accountability.

## DeFi Thesis

Axodus DeFi is not a guaranteed yield engine.

Axodus DeFi is a risk-aware, governance-aligned, treasury-integrated, user-educational, and modular decentralized finance layer.

Its purpose is to support sustainable DeFi products and services with:

- transparent risk classification;
- governance-controlled parameters;
- treasury visibility;
- smart contract review;
- user disclosures;
- accountability reports;
- educational onboarding;
- productized DeFi services;
- DaaS and ETF-oriented design concepts.

## Strategic Direction

Axodus DeFi should be documented as a structured DeFi infrastructure nucleus, not as a collection of speculative farming opportunities.

The main strategic direction is DaaS: DeFi as a Service.

DaaS means packaging DeFi capabilities into controlled, explainable, governable, and service-oriented products.

ETF DaaS is a deeper design direction: an ETF-oriented DeFi-as-a-Service model intended to represent diversified, risk-aware, governable DeFi exposure.

Important: ETF DaaS must not be described as a regulated ETF unless legally and formally established. It should be framed as an ETF-oriented DeFi model or design direction, subject to governance, legal, contract, treasury, and risk review.

## Non-Negotiable DeFi Invariants

- DeFi documentation must never promise guaranteed APY, guaranteed yield, guaranteed profit, or risk-free returns.
- DaaS means DeFi as a Service.
- ETF DaaS must not be described as a regulated ETF unless legally established.
- DeFi products must be governance-aligned.
- DeFi strategies involving treasury assets require treasury policy, risk limits, governance review, and reporting.
- User-facing DeFi products require clear disclosures and education.
- Smart contracts and protocol integrations require security review before production.
- External protocol risks must be disclosed.
- Yield estimates must be labeled as variable, contextual, and non-guaranteed.
- Derivatives and debentures are sensitive concepts and must be documented as planned/conceptual unless legally, technically, and governance validated.
- ACS supports analysis but does not approve or execute DeFi actions alone.
- Public DeFi documentation must be written in English.
