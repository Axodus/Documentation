# BBA Platform Mission Rules

## Purpose

Define the permanent rules that make a Mission a valid and accountable unit of
domain work. These rules apply across Tenants and Solutions unless a later
approved domain policy explicitly narrows or delegates their application.

## Mission Identity and Context

1. Every Mission belongs to exactly one Tenant context at a time.
2. Every Mission has an institutional objective, a responsible Steward, and a
   declared purpose that can be understood without implementation knowledge.
3. A Mission must identify its intended audience or explicitly record why an
   audience is not applicable.
4. A Mission must declare relevant context, assumptions, constraints, and
   expected outcomes before it can be treated as authorized.
5. Tenant-specific branding, terminology, or Solution framing cannot change the
   canonical meaning of Mission.
6. An Axodus Mission is an instance of the same domain rules as any other
   Tenant Mission.

## Accountability and Authority

1. A Steward is accountable for Mission direction, scope, review routing, and
   outcome acceptance within delegated authority.
2. Human Governance retains authority for consequential approvals, review
   decisions, escalations, and Publication authorization.
3. An AI Workforce member may recommend, research, create, transform, or
   evaluate work, but its output does not constitute human approval.
4. A Reviewer may require revision or escalation when correctness, evidence,
   quality, safety, rights, or policy is inadequate.
5. Publication Authority may withhold publication even when a Mission result is
   accepted for internal use.
6. No role may grant itself authority by producing an Asset, recommendation,
   Review, or Publication candidate.

## Work and Asset Rules

1. Mission work must remain related to its declared objective or be explicitly
   redirected through a human decision.
2. Every Institutional Asset produced or transformed within a Mission retains
   provenance to that Mission and its responsible context.
3. An Asset draft is not accepted merely because it is complete, polished, or
   generated by an agent.
4. Acceptance, Publication authorization, and distribution are distinct
   decisions.
5. An Asset with unresolved material claims, rights, safety, or domain concerns
   cannot be treated as accepted without an explicit authority decision and
   recorded limitation.
6. A Publication must identify the accepted Asset, intended audience,
   destination, and responsible Publication Authority.
7. Distribution of an authorized Asset cannot expand its authorization to an
   unrelated audience or destination.

## Review Rules

1. Review depth must correspond to the Asset, claim, audience, risk, and
   destination context.
2. Review findings must distinguish correction, acceptance, rejection,
   escalation, and unresolved uncertainty.
3. A Mission may not bypass a required review by changing the Asset class,
   audience label, or Solution name.
4. A review recommendation is not an approval unless the responsible authority
   explicitly accepts it.
5. Conflicting domain authorities require escalation rather than silent
   selection of the most convenient result.

## Outcome Rules

1. Every Mission conclusion must classify its result as achieved, advanced,
   disproved, deferred, redirected, rejected, stopped, or otherwise explicitly
   unresolved.
2. Closure requires a human outcome decision, relevant limitations, residual
   obligations, and available learning.
3. Publication is not required for closure.
4. A safe refusal, prevented release, clarified decision, or useful knowledge
   may be a valid Mission outcome.
5. Activity volume, Asset count, or Publication count is not sufficient evidence
   of Mission success.
6. A Mission result cannot be treated as a guaranteed business, scientific,
   financial, reputational, or audience outcome.

## Tenant and Knowledge Rules

1. Tenant knowledge, policies, Assets, authority, and decisions remain scoped to
   that Tenant unless an explicit authorized sharing relationship exists.
2. One Tenant's Steward, Reviewer, or Publication Authority cannot authorize
   another Tenant's Mission or Publication by default.
3. Shared or external knowledge must retain its source responsibility and
   applicable usage limits.
4. Tenant context must not silently become a permanent Core rule.
5. A Solution may specialize Mission work but cannot redefine Mission identity,
   authority, Asset, Review, or Publication semantics.

## Change and Provenance Rules

1. A material change to objective, scope, audience, authority, constraints, or
   expected outcome requires an explicit Mission decision.
2. A material change must preserve the reason, responsible authority, affected
   Assets, and consequences for review or Publication.
3. Reusing an Asset in another Mission does not transfer ownership of the
   original Mission's decisions or authority.
4. Superseding an Asset or outcome must preserve the relationship to the prior
   context and the reason for supersession.
5. A reopened Mission must retain its prior outcome and identify the authority
   and reason for reopening.

## Rule Precedence

When rules appear to conflict, apply the following order:

1. explicit Human Governance and authority decisions;
2. Tenant policies and applicable domain constraints;
3. Mission-specific scope and objective;
4. general Mission rules;
5. agent recommendations or Solution conventions.

No lower-level recommendation can override a higher-level authority, policy,
or constraint.

## Technical Boundary

These rules do not define validation code, database constraints, API behavior,
workflow engine logic, event handlers, access-control mechanisms, or runtime
enforcement. Implementations must realize the rules without changing their
semantic precedence.
