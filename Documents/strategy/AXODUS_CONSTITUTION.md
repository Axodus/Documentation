# AXODUS CONSTITUTION

Version: 0.1 Draft  
Status: Working Document  
Governance Model: DAO Federation  
Root Authority: Axodus Root DAO

---

## 1. Preamble

Axodus exists to establish a governed, decentralized and autonomous economic network powered by DAOs, digital assets, autonomous agents, decentralized infrastructure and collective governance.

This Constitution defines the foundational principles, operational limits, rights, responsibilities and governance standards that regulate the Axodus ecosystem and all DAOs operating within it.

All DAOs connected to Axodus must explicitly accept and operate under this Constitution to gain access to ecosystem products, contracts, treasury integrations and economic infrastructure.

---

## 2. Core Values

### 2.1 Sovereignty
Every DAO possesses operational autonomy within the limits established by this Constitution.

### 2.2 Transparency
Treasury actions, proposals, allocations and governance decisions must be auditable.

### 2.3 Sustainability
Axodus prioritizes real revenue, long-term capital preservation and sustainable economic expansion over short-term speculation.

### 2.4 Alignment
All DAOs must operate in alignment with Axodus values, economic goals and governance standards.

### 2.5 Education Before Access
Users should understand product risks, mechanics and responsibilities before accessing advanced ecosystem products.

### 2.6 Governance as Execution
Governance is not only voting. Governance is the coordination and execution layer of the ecosystem.

---

## 3. Axodus Root DAO

The Axodus Root DAO is responsible for:

- maintaining the Constitution;
- governing ecosystem-wide parameters;
- managing Root Treasury policies;
- approving protocol integrations;
- defining governance standards;
- protecting ecosystem integrity;
- managing expansion policies;
- defining product access rules.

---

## 4. Partner DAOs

A Partner DAO is an autonomous economic unit created, configured and operated inside the Axodus ecosystem.

A Partner DAO may represent:

- an individual operator;
- a company;
- an investment group;
- a community;
- a service provider;
- a trading group;
- a mining operation;
- an agency;
- an autonomous business unit.

---

## 5. Requirements for Participation

A DAO must:

- accept the Axodus Constitution;
- use approved governance contracts;
- respect treasury rules;
- respect ecosystem security standards;
- follow proposal execution policies;
- operate transparently;
- comply with product-specific minimum requirements.

---

## 6. DAO Rights

Partner DAOs may:

- manage their own treasury;
- define internal governance;
- onboard clients and members;
- allocate capital between Axodus products;
- operate approved services;
- generate revenue;
- deploy internal strategies;
- use ecosystem infrastructure;
- access Marketplace, Academy, MCP Servers, Compute, Mining, Trading and DeFi products when eligible.

---

## 7. DAO Responsibilities

Partner DAOs must:

- respect constitutional limits;
- maintain operational transparency;
- avoid malicious activities;
- protect member funds;
- respect governance execution rules;
- maintain treasury accountability;
- disclose product risks to their members when applicable.

---

## 8. Product Access

Axodus products should be accessed through DAO governance and treasury execution whenever the product involves capital allocation, treasury movement, shared responsibility or federated operation.

Products include:

- Mining;
- Trading;
- Staking / DeFi;
- Marketplace;
- Academy;
- MCP Servers;
- Compute Network;
- DEX;
- Vaults;
- Axodus BBA;
- Lottery, when activated.

---

## 9. Governance Model

### Root Governance
Ecosystem-level decisions, constitutional amendments, product rules and treasury policies.

### DAO Governance
Local DAO decisions, treasury allocation, member rules, product participation and internal operations.

---

## 10. Proposal Execution

Recommended lifecycle:

1. Proposal creation
2. Discussion
3. Voting
4. Approval or rejection
5. Timelock, when applicable
6. Execution
7. Receipt and reporting

---

## 11. Economic Principles

Axodus economic activity must be oriented toward:

- sustainable treasury growth;
- capital preservation;
- productive revenue;
- reinvestment;
- transparency;
- efficient administration;
- alignment between holders, DAOs, builders and users.

---

## 12. $Neurons

$Neurons may serve as:

- governance asset;
- incentive asset;
- access coordination asset;
- Proof of Knowledge reward;
- license acquisition asset;
- ecosystem alignment mechanism.

$Neurons should not be treated as an inflationary subsidy mechanism without productive backing.

---

## 13. Autonomous Agents

Agents may operate inside the ecosystem under governance supervision.

Examples:

- Morpheus — strategy and governance alignment;
- Agent Smith — risk, adversarial review and integrity;
- Trinity — trading, execution and operational systems;
- RedHat — development, infrastructure and technical coordination.

---

## 14. Security and Emergency Controls

The ecosystem may define mechanisms to:

- suspend malicious DAOs;
- revoke integrations;
- restrict unsafe product access;
- trigger emergency review;
- pause risky operations when governance-approved rules allow it.

---

## 15. Constitutional Amendments

This Constitution may only be modified through Root DAO governance.

---

## 16. Final Principle

Axodus exists to enable autonomous economic coordination through governance, technology, education, productive capital and collective intelligence.
