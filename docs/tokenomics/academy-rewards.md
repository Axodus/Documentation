## Purpose

Academy is a major reward and utility source for `$Neurons`. Through Learn-to-Win and Proof of Knowledge, Academy can reward meaningful learning and onboarding.

Academy rewards are also one of the highest abuse-risk tokenomics surfaces.

## Scope

This page covers free courses, paid courses, certifications, partner programs, Learn-to-Win, Proof of Knowledge, locked or internal-use rewards, anti-abuse controls, and reporting requirements.

## Reward Contexts

Free courses support onboarding, basic education, risk awareness, and initial user progression. Free-course rewards may be smaller and may be locked or internal-use if supported by policy and contracts.

Paid courses support advanced learning, premium content, and tutor or partner course economy. Paid-course rewards may follow different rules, but they are not automatically liquid.

Certifications may signal skill or readiness. They should not be framed as legal, profit, or financial claims.

Partner programs require Business, Governance, and Tokenomics review when material.

## Learn-to-Win Tokenomics

Learn-to-Win is a gamified learning reward model linking progress, assessment, and token or internal utility. It should reward learning, not clicks. Meaningful rewards should require Proof of Knowledge, budget limits, anti-abuse controls, and reporting.

## Proof of Knowledge Relationship

Proof of Knowledge validates learning before reward eligibility, reduces superficial completion, supports certification or progression, and improves anti-farming resistance.

## Free Course Reward Flow

A free-course flow may include enrollment, progress record, Proof of Knowledge or assessment, anti-abuse checks, reward policy resolution, locked or internal-use issuance if supported, restricted utility, and reward budget reporting.

Do not claim free rewards can be withdrawn or sold unless implementation supports it.

## Paid Course Reward Flow

A paid-course flow may include purchase or access validation, completion, Proof of Knowledge, reward policy resolution, locked/unlocked/partial reward issuance under policy, certification or access update, and Marketplace or reward budget reporting.

Paid-course reward behavior may differ from free-course rewards, but it still requires policy and implementation.

## Policy Fields and Controls

Academy reward policies should define course ID, course type, learning path, reward amount or formula, proof required, reward status, lock status, internal use, claim rules, unlock conditions, limits, anti-abuse rules, budget source, governance status, contract reference, and reporting frequency.

Controls may include Proof of Knowledge, per-user limits, per-wallet limits if policy allows, attempt limits, cooldowns, suspicious completion detection, ACS risk analysis, manual review, self-dealing detection, and governance review for large campaigns.

## Risks

Risks include reward farming, sybil attacks, credential inflation, free rewards becoming market sell pressure, tutor self-dealing, and contract mismatch.
