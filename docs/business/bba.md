## Purpose

BBA means Blockchain Business & Advertising.

Axodus BBA is a specialized blockchain-native business communication and advertising layer. It supports product positioning, campaign strategy, growth operations, launch communication, AI-assisted advertising workflows, and ROI-aware measurement without using hype or misleading claims.

## Scope

BBA sits inside the Business runtime and connects with ACS, Marketplace, Academy, Governance, and product nuclei. It is not a generic ad agency, token pump channel, fake urgency engine, or unreviewed public-claim channel.

## Service Areas

- Product positioning: audience definition, value proposition, market context, and narrative framework.
- Campaign strategy: objectives, channel strategy, message maps, content plans, conversion paths, and measurement plans.
- Growth operations: acquisition funnels, onboarding flows, retention loops, referral or community strategy, and performance dashboard requirements.
- Blockchain business communication: token utility language, governance story, treasury transparency language, and DeFi risk-aware messaging.
- AI-assisted content: ad copy drafts, content variants, campaign briefs, social content, and landing page messaging.
- Launch support: announcement plans, launch checklists, documentation alignment, and community update plans.
- Brand architecture: naming, sub-brand relationships, ecosystem mapping, and message guidelines.

## BBA Intake Fields

BBA requests should capture requester, product or brand, campaign objective, target audience, product status, claims to be made, token or financial elements, DAO or governance context, channels, budget or resource model, timeline, success metrics, legal or compliance constraints, existing assets, required deliverables, and approval workflow.

## Campaign Runtime

1. Intake: collect product context, objectives, audience, and constraints.
2. Product truth review: verify what is live, planned, experimental, or conceptual.
3. Claims review: identify financial, token, audit, partnership, performance, or governance claims.
4. Strategy definition: define positioning, channels, funnel, and success metrics.
5. ACS-assisted generation: produce drafts, variants, and campaign analysis.
6. Human review: validate accuracy, tone, risk, and brand fit.
7. Governance or policy review: escalate material token, governance, DAO, treasury, partnership, or official-status claims.
8. Campaign execution: publish or launch under approved scope.
9. Measurement: collect metrics and feedback.
10. Reporting: report results, limitations, and next steps.

## Claim Review

BBA may describe verified product status, token utility, access mechanics, rewards, governance participation, and contextual historical metrics when properly supported. It must not claim live functionality that is not implemented, guaranteed appreciation, guaranteed income, guaranteed trading performance, unsupported APY, fake audits, or unconfirmed partnerships.

## Metrics

BBA may track awareness, engagement, conversion, retention, business, and governance metrics. Metrics should be presented with context and limitations. They must not be used to imply unsupported causality or guaranteed future results.

## ACS Support

ACS may assist audience research, message variants, content calendars, claim risk detection, narrative alignment, market context summaries, and campaign performance summaries. Human review is required before publication.

## Governance Touchpoints

Governance or policy review may be required for token campaigns, governance campaigns, DAO announcements, treasury or financial performance claims, product access policy, official Axodus status claims, federated DAO campaigns, partnership announcements, or ecosystem-level messages.

## Risks

BBA risks include hype, token marketing misrepresentation, product-readiness mismatch, compliance uncertainty, reputation damage, AI-generated false claims, and ROI misrepresentation.
