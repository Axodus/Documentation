## Purpose

Axodus DeFi is the risk-aware decentralized finance nucleus for DeFi-as-a-Service, protocol integrations, vault and strategy research, treasury DeFi support, user-facing DeFi products, and accountability-driven reporting.

## Scope

DeFi covers DaaS, ETF-oriented DaaS, protocol integrations, vault-like structures, staking and yield models, liquidity models, derivatives and debenture concepts, risk management, smart contract security, governance alignment, treasury alignment, tokenomics alignment, Academy education, ACS support, and public disclosures.

Axodus DeFi is not a guaranteed-yield platform, passive-income promise, risk-free product, unreviewed farming engine, regulated ETF, or substitute for user education, security review, or governance.

## Ecosystem Role

DeFi is the protocol and DaaS product layer for Axodus. It translates decentralized finance opportunities into scoped, risk-reviewed, governable, educational, and reportable products or services.

## Operating Direction

DaaS is the primary operating direction: packaging DeFi capabilities into structured, scoped, governable, and reportable services. ETF DaaS is a design direction for diversified, ETF-oriented DeFi exposure, but it must not be described as a regulated ETF unless legal, regulatory, governance, and product structures establish that status.

## Relationships

DeFi connects with [Governance](../governance/overview.md) for protocol and strategy approval, [Treasury](../treasury/overview.md) for capital exposure and reporting, [Tokenomics](../tokenomics/overview.md) for future utility and incentives, [Academy](../academy/overview.md) for user education, [Business](../business/overview.md) for DaaS service intake, [Trading](../trading/overview.md) for strategy and liquidity context, [ACS](../acs/overview.md) for analysis, and [Accountability](../accountability/overview.md) for records.

## Runtime Summary

1. A DeFi request, product, protocol, or strategy is identified.
2. The product category and user or treasury exposure are classified.
3. Risk level, protocol dependencies, contract dependencies, and disclosures are defined.
4. ACS may support analysis, but cannot approve or execute DeFi actions.
5. Treasury, governance, security, legal, or compliance review is routed when required.
6. Approved work is implemented or documented under scope.
7. Monitoring, reporting, and accountability records are maintained.

## Risk Considerations

DeFi products involve smart contract, protocol, market, liquidity, oracle, bridge, governance, custody, user-understanding, and regulatory risk. APY, rewards, yield, and returns are variable and must never be presented as guaranteed.
