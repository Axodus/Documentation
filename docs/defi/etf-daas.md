## Purpose

ETF DaaS means ETF-oriented DeFi as a Service. It is a design direction for diversified, rule-based, governable, and risk-aware DeFi exposure.

## Scope

ETF DaaS may involve baskets, allocation rules, rebalancing policy, risk limits, reporting, and governance control. It is not documented as a regulated ETF, security, fund, or guaranteed return product.

## Conceptual Components

- Basket: a collection of assets, protocols, or strategies.
- Allocation rules: how weights are assigned.
- Rebalancing policy: how allocations change over time.
- Risk limits: exposure, liquidity, protocol, or asset limits.
- Theme: an index-style thesis such as stable DeFi, blue-chip DeFi, liquidity basket, or DAO treasury basket.
- Reporting: disclosure of exposure, changes, risks, and limitations.

## Governance Requirements

Governance review is required to create an ETF DaaS basket, add or remove protocols, change allocation rules, change rebalancing policy, use treasury capital, make a user-facing product live, change risk limits, publish performance claims, or use regulated product language.

## Risk Considerations

Diversified does not mean safe. ETF DaaS may introduce regulatory language risk, correlated protocol risk, rebalancing risk, liquidity risk, protocol risk, and reporting risk.
